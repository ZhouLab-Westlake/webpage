Research

One question, many lenses.

Life is organized across scales — from a whole nucleus down to a single nucleosome. We develop and combine cryo-electron tomography, artificial intelligence, biochemical reconstitution and cellular experiments to watch biomolecules organize inside and outside the cell, and to ask how that organization runs the genome and shapes what a cell becomes.

Theme 01

Chromatin, organized across scales

How does a two-metre genome fold into a nucleus, and how does that folding decide which genes are read? We connect nucleosome spacing and chromatin fiber folding to the genome's 3D architecture — and to how it is tuned.

Theme 02

Biomolecular condensates

Many biomolecules condense into membraneless compartments that concentrate reactions and organize the crowded interior of the cell. We study how these condensates form, what they gather, and how they tune genome activity.

Theme 03

From structure to cell fate

Ultimately, organization matters because it changes what cells do. We link molecular organization to transcription, genome function, and the decisions that set a cell's identity.
